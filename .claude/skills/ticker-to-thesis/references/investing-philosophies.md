# Six Investing Philosophies

Each analyst embodies a distinct worldview. They're not just different prompts—they're different mental models.

# Quality Compounders

**Philosophy**: "Far better to buy a wonderful business at a fair price than a fair business at a wonderful price."

**Time Horizon**: Forever

**Primary Edge**: Moat durability

**Key Metric**: ROIC

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- Durable competitive advantages ("moats") that compound intrinsic value over decades
- Owner-operator mentality: think like business owners, not stock traders
- Pricing power, brand strength, and reinvestment runways
- Hold forever — selling is the exception, not the rule

---

## The Mental Model

Can this business look fundamentally similar — and stronger — in 20 years? If yes, buy and hold. If the moat erodes, reassess.

---

## Representatives

Warren Buffett, Charlie Munger, Li Lu, Duan Yongping

# Imaginative Growth

**Philosophy**: "The biggest investment mistakes are failures of imagination."

**Time Horizon**: 5+ years

**Primary Edge**: Imagination / TAM vision

**Key Metric**: Revenue growth

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- Asks "What could go very, very right?" rather than stress-testing downside first
- Embraces volatility as price movement, not risk — conviction survives drawdowns
- 5+ year minimum holding periods; think in decades
- TAM vision: the market systematically underestimates the duration and magnitude of transformational growth
- Crossover fluency: public/private boundaries are arbitrary; follow the innovation cycle

---

## The Mental Model

Spend the first 30 minutes on any stock discussing only the upside. If the imagination case isn't compelling, move on. Failures come from not dreaming big enough, not from dreaming too big.

---

## The Spectrum

- **Long-duration conviction**: Baillie Gifford — early Amazon ($40), Tesla ($38), Nvidia
- **VC crossover**: Sequoia (SCGE), a16z — participate in the entire innovation lifecycle
- **Paradigm-defining bets**: Cathie Wood / ARK — concentrated positions in open-ended, exponential themes (AI, genomics, autonomous systems)

# Fundamental Long/Short (Tiger Cubs)

**Philosophy**: "Use fundamental research to buy the best companies and short the worst companies." — Julian Robertson

**Time Horizon**: 1-3 years

**Primary Edge**: Variant perception + hedging

**Key Metric**: EV/EBITDA

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- **Shorter horizon** (1-3 years) vs. growth investors' 5+ years — edge is on timing, not just vision
- **They hedge**: short the losers, not just buy winners — profit from both sides
- **Variant perception**: deep diligence reveals what consensus is missing, then trade it
- **Risk management discipline**: quick exits from bad positions (Robertson's legacy)
- TMT skew: the sector where information edge compounds fastest

Both Tigers and Imaginative Growth love high-growth tech. The difference: Tigers trade around it with hedges and faster turnover; growth investors sit through volatility.

---

## The Mental Model

What does 100 hours of diligence reveal that the market is missing? Size the long. Find the structurally impaired competitor. Size the short. Manage the spread.

# Deep Value

**Philosophy**: "I will buy what other people are selling, what is out of favour, what is loathed and despised."

**Time Horizon**: Years (patient)

**Primary Edge**: Margin of safety

**Key Metric**: Replacement cost

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- **Margin of safety** is paramount — buy at prices that discount catastrophe
- **Patient, contrarian, benchmark-agnostic** — no catalyst required; price eventually converges to value
- **Second-level thinking on cycles** (Howard Marks): when everyone agrees something is risky, that consensus *reduces* risk through lower prices
- Comfortable with complex, ugly, or orphaned securities others avoid
- Distinct from event-driven: no need to identify a catalyst; time and mean-reversion do the work

---

## The Mental Model

What would a private buyer pay for this entire business? If the public market offers a 40%+ discount to that figure, buy and wait.

# Event-Driven / Catalyst

**Philosophy**: Identify or create catalysts that unlock value independent of market direction.

**Time Horizon**: 6-18 months

**Primary Edge**: Catalyst identification

**Key Metric**: Catalyst timeline

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- **Catalyst is the edge**: spin-offs, restructurings, bankruptcy emergence, proxy fights
- **Time-bound opportunities**: 6-18 month windows tied to corporate actions
- Two modes of operation:
  - *Special Situations*: find existing catalysts (Tepper, Paulson)
  - *Activist*: manufacture catalysts through engagement (Icahn, Ackman, Elliott)
- Unifying thread: both profit from events, not just valuation convergence

---

## The Mental Model

What specific, identifiable event will force the market to re-price this security? If you can't name the catalyst, you're not event-driven — you're just hoping.

# Macro-Tactical (Druckenmiller School)

**Philosophy**: "Earnings don't move the overall market; it's the Federal Reserve Board."

**Time Horizon**: Regime-dependent

**Primary Edge**: Liquidity + top-down

**Key Metric**: Fed policy

---

## What Makes It Distinct

- **Liquidity is the lens, the Fed is the sun**: macro regime determines the playing field
- **50/30/20 framework**: 50% of a stock's move is the overall market, 30% is sector, 20% is idiosyncratic — get macro right, and stock-picking mistakes are forgiven
- **Anticipate changes not expected by others**: second-order thinking on macro shifts before they're consensus
- **Concentration when right**: asymmetric bet sizing — "be a pig when you're right"
- **Trade the entire board**: stocks, bonds, currencies, commodities — flexible across instruments
- **Pattern recognition from Soros**: reflexivity, understanding how market psychology creates self-fulfilling loops

Not just a "good PM" — a specific mental model for synthesizing top-down macro with bottom-up stock selection.

---

## The Mental Model

What is liquidity doing? Which sectors benefit from this regime? What's the best expression of the theme? How much conviction warrants how much size?

---

## Track Record Context

30% average annual return over three decades at Duquesne, without a single down year.

---

Stanley Druckenmiller, Scott Bessent
