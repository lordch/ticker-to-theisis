# Prognozy inwestycji budowlanych w Polsce na 2026 rok - Inzynier Budownictwa

## Overall Investment Volume
- **Total planned projects:** 10,115 investments
- **Total value:** Over 503 billion PLN across four quarters
- **Assessment:** "znaczacy wzrost aktywnosci" (significant growth in activity) compared to 2 years of slowdown

## Investment by Segment

### Residential (Mieszkaniowy)
- **Projects planned:** 3,627
- **Total value:** ~123.6 billion PLN
- **Key regions:** Mazovia (648 projects, 28.8B PLN), Silesia (350), Malopolska (332)
- **Q2 growth:** 64% quarter-over-quarter increase expected
- **Drivers:** Interest rate stabilization, mortgage guarantee programs, developer activity

### Non-Residential (Niemieszkaniowy)
- **Projects planned:** Multiple sectors
- **Total value:** ~173.5 billion PLN
- **Key focus:** Nearly 40% from industrial/logistics projects driven by nearshoring trends
- **Strong regions:** Masovia, Silesia, Pomerania, Greater Poland

### Infrastructure/Engineering (Inzynieryjny)
- **Market share:** Over 40% of total investment value
- **Total value:** ~206 billion PLN
- **Project types:** Roads, railways, hydroelectric, energy networks
- **Leading region:** Pomerania (record 91.6B PLN), focused on maritime/energy infrastructure

## Key Growth Drivers

1. Improved municipal liquidity and interest rate stability
2. EU fund absorption from 2021-2027 perspective
3. Lower commercial project financing costs
4. Government infrastructure programs: GDDKiA road expansions (500 km additional), PKP railways (9.5B PLN in tenders)
5. Energy infrastructure expansion (66.3B PLN planned through 2037)

## Major Projects Highlighted
- S12 highway section (Piaski-Dorohusk): 2.6B PLN
- Rail modernization (E30 line, Subcarpathia): 1.8B PLN
- Gdynia external port expansion: 3B+ PLN

## Risk Factors
- German industrial slowdown affecting manufacturing sector
- Material cost volatility
- Labor availability constraints
- Administrative approval delays

## Market Outlook 2026-2027
- Predicted annual investment growth: **8-10%**
- Strongest segment: Infrastructure and energy
- Moderate segment: Residential (metropolitan focus)
- Slowest growth: Commercial/office (market correction phase)
